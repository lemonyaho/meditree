07 Congenital Anomalies
07.1 Hypospadia
07.1.1 Definition
→ Abnormal openings of urethra along inferior aspect of penis

07.1.2 Developmental mechanism
→ Incomplete fusion of urethral folds

07.1.3 Incidence
→ 3~5 / 1000

07.1.4 Etiology / risk factors
→ Unknown
→ Environmental factors
→ Estrogens
→ Endocrine disruptors

07.2 Epispadia
07.2.1 Definition
→ Urethral meatus on dorsum of penis

07.2.2 Incidence
→ 1 / 30,000

07.2.3 Developmental mechanism
→ Genital tubercle forms in region of urorectal septum

07.2.4 Cloacal membrane position
→ Cranial to genital tubercle

07.2.5 Most Often Associated with
→ Exstrophy of bladder

07.3 Exstrophy of the Bladder
07.3.1 Definition
→ Bladder mucosa exposed to the outside

07.3.2 Anatomical defect
→ Defect of abdominal wall ventral to bladder

07.3.3 Developmental mechanism
→ Failure of migration of primitive streak mesoderm to around cloacal membrane

07.3.4 Associated anomaly
→ Epispadia

07.4 Exstrophy of the Cloaca
07.4.1 Related spectrum
→ Epispadia and exstrophy of cloaca
